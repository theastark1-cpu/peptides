⛔ Critical Safety for 60-Year-Old Men on Tesamorelin:

1. Baseline PSA + IGF-1 bloodwork before starting. Repeat at week 8.
2. Start at 500 mcg (15 units) for first 5–7 days to test histamine tolerance.
3. If IGF-1 exceeds age-adjusted upper normal (typically ~200 ng/mL for 60yo), reduce dose or extend washout.
4. Any PSA elevation → stop immediately, investigate.
5. 5/2 cycling is mandatory — preserves pituitary sensitivity, prevents IGF-1 creep.
6. Never combine Tesamorelin with other peptides in the same syringe.
⚠ Histamine Warning: Tesamorelin can trigger histamine release — full-body hives, redness, itching. Not an allergy to the peptide itself, but reaction to the hexenoic acid modification or mannitol excipient. Management: Start with half-dose (500 mcg / 15 units) for first 3 days; pre-medicate with 25–50 mg diphenhydramine 30 min prior if sensitive.

⚡ Why MOTS-c for a 60-year-old athlete? MOTS-c is an endogenous mitochondrial peptide that declines with age. It activates the AMPK pathway (metformin-like glucose disposal + fat oxidation), improves physical performance in old mice by 12–15%, and correlates with greater muscle power in aging humans. Unlike Tesamorelin, MOTS-c doesn't touch the GH/IGF-1 axis — cleaner safety profile. This is your primary lever for the 20 lbs alongside Tesa.
⚠ MOTS-c Precautions:
• Avoid combining with metformin or other AMPK activators (redundant pathways).
• Heart palpitations are the most commonly reported side effect — usually transient (first 2 weeks). If persistent, drop to 3mg.
• Contraindicated if active cancer is present — AMPK activation can be pro-growth in some cancer lines.
• Injection site can sting — draw slow, massage after.

FAQ

Q: Why not just use straight HGH instead of Tesamorelin?

Tesamorelin is a GHRH analog — it stimulates the pituitary to release your own GH in pulses, preserving the natural feedback loop. Straight HGH floods the system, suppresses endogenous production, and has a worse IGF-1 overshoot profile. For a 60-year-old, the cleaner GH pulse from Tesa is safer — especially for prostate health.

Q: Can BPC-157 be taken orally instead of injected?

Oral BPC-157 (Arg-BPC capsules) works well for gut-specific issues but doesn't distribute systemically to joints and tendons. For a mountain biker, subQ is strongly recommended — it reaches the knees, shoulders, and wrists where he needs it. If gut health is also a concern, you can add oral BPC on top.

Q: Is MOTS-c worth it, or can we skip it?

For a 60-year-old trying to lose 20 lbs while maintaining athletic performance, MOTS-c is the highest-leverage peptide in the stack after the cognitive shield. It works on metabolic flexibility — the root cause of age-related weight gain — rather than just appetite or calories. If you had to drop one, drop TB-500 before MOTS-c. But ideally, run all 5.

Q: Does he need lab work for all of these?

Tesamorelin is the only one requiring lab monitoring (IGF-1, PSA, glucose). Semax, BPC-157, TB-500, and MOTS-c are non-hormonal and don't require specific labs. That said, a baseline metabolic panel and CBC at 60 is just good practice regardless.

Q: What about the dementia family history — is Semax enough?

Semax is the daily shield — it upregulates BDNF, provides neuroprotection, and has direct Alzheimer's model evidence. But if dementia is the #1 concern, the Phase 2 additions (Cerebrolycin + Epitalon) are worth serious consideration. Cerebrolysin is clinical-grade neuroprotection. At minimum, one Cerebrolysin cycle per year starting at 60 is a very reasonable insurance policy.
