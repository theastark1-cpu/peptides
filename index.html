Reconstitution: Add 3.0 mL bacteriostatic water to the 10 mg vial. Swirl gently — do not shake. Refrigerate at 2–8°C.

Dosing: 400 mcg (12 units) once daily in the morning. Can be taken with or without food.

Cycling: No tolerance buildup — continuous daily use is standard. Optional: 10–20 day courses with equal off-periods. This protocol: daily, year-round. Skip on weekends if desired.

Benefits: A Russian nootropic peptide — an analog of ACTH(4-7) with a C-terminal Pro-Gly-Pro extension for stability. Upregulates BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor), improves focus, mental clarity, and neuroplasticity. Provides neuroprotection against oxidative stress. While not directly targeting sleep, gut, or hair, cognitive enhancement supports overall wellbeing and indirectly benefits all systems. This peptide is included for brain health — a bonus fourth pillar of the protocol.

Syringe Math: 3.33 mg/mL ÷ 100 units/mL = 33.3 mcg/unit. 400 mcg ÷ 33.3 = 12 units.

Reconstitution: Add 3.0 mL bacteriostatic water. Swirl gently. Refrigerate at 2–8°C.

Dosing: 1 mg (30 units) once daily, in the evening. Inject at least 90 minutes after the last meal and right before bed. 5 days on, 2 days off. Start with a 500 mcg (15 unit) test dose for the first 2–3 days.

Benefits: FDA-approved GHRH analog that drives robust, sustained GH release. Improves deep sleep quality, accelerates gut barrier repair, and stimulates hair follicle anagen (growth) phase.

⚠ Histamine Warning: Tesamorelin can trigger histamine release in sensitive individuals — causing full-body hives, redness, and itching. This is a known reaction (not an allergy to the peptide itself, but to its hexenoic acid modification or the mannitol excipient). If this occurs: take 25–50 mg Benadryl (diphenhydramine); symptoms typically resolve within 20–30 minutes. Start with a half-dose (500 mcg / 15 units) for the first 3 days to test tolerance. Pre-medicating with an antihistamine 30 minutes before injection may prevent the reaction entirely.

Reconstitution: Add 2.0 mL bacteriostatic water. Swirl very gently — GHK-Cu is fragile. The solution will have a distinctive blue tint (normal — it's the copper). Refrigerate at 2–8°C, protected from light.

Dosing: 1 mg (20 units) once daily, morning preferred. Can cycle 30 days on / 2 weeks off, or run continuously. Some protocols escalate to 2 mg/day after the first 2 weeks.

⚠ Injection Site Stinging: GHK-Cu is mildly acidic (pH ~5.5) and the copper ion itself can cause a stinging sensation on injection. Solutions: (1) inject very slowly over 10–15 seconds; (2) dilute further with an extra 1 mL BAC water (3 mL total → 3.33 mg/mL → 30 units per 1 mg dose); (3) apply an ice cube to the site for 30 seconds before injection; (4) some users add 0.1 mL of preservative-free lidocaine (prescription required). The sting is harmless and fades within 30–60 seconds.

Crosses the blood-brain barrier and modulates gene expression inside neurons — activating anti-apoptotic pathways, synaptic plasticity genes, and cellular maintenance programs. Research shows a 71% increase in functional dendritic spines (the physical basis of memory). Not a stimulant — benefits are structural, cumulative, and build across cycles. Pairs beautifully with Semax: Semax gives fast BDNF-driven cognitive lift; Pinealon builds the underlying neurological infrastructure.

💉 Combined Draw (PM): Pinealon (50u) + DSIP (6u) = 56 units total in one syringe. Draw DSIP first (smaller volume), then Pinealon. Both are evening peptides targeting complementary sleep/circadian pathways. Tesamorelin must remain separate — different pH and receptor pathways.

Syringe Math: 10 mg/mL. 5 mg dose = 0.5 mL = 50 units. Combined with DSIP (6u) = 56 units total on a U-100 insulin syringe.

Supplies Checklist

  • U-100 insulin syringes — 31G, 5/16" or 8mm needle, 1 mL capacity. Estimate ~35 syringes/week (5–6 per day × 7 days minus off-days). Buy in boxes of 100.
  • Bacteriostatic water — 30 mL multi-dose vials. Estimate 2–3 bottles for initial reconstitution of all 6 peptides (14 mL total with Pinealon's 1 mL reconstitution). Keep a spare.
  • Alcohol swabs — 2 per injection (vial stopper wipe + skin prep). ~12–14 per day. Buy bulk boxes of 200+.
  • Sharps container — FDA-approved, puncture-resistant. Available at any pharmacy.
  • Vial storage case — Insulin vial organizer or small cosmetic fridge organizer to keep reconstituted vials upright and organized in the refrigerator.
  • Extra mixing syringes — 3 mL syringes with 21G–23G needles for BAC water transfer. Do not use insulin syringes for reconstitution — the fine needle damages the peptide.
  • Labels / Sharpie — Label every vial with reconstitution date and concentration immediately.
  • Refrigerator thermometer — Verify fridge stays at 2–8°C (35–46°F). Peptides degrade rapidly above 8°C.
  • Injection log / notebook — Track daily doses, injection sites, and any reactions or effects.

Injection Technique

  1. Wash hands thoroughly with soap and warm water.
  2. Wipe vial stopper with an alcohol swab. Let dry completely (10–15 seconds).
  3. Draw air into the syringe equal to your dose volume. Inject air into the vial to equalize pressure.
  4. Draw the dose slowly. Flick the syringe to dislodge bubbles, then push plunger gently to expel air. Confirm exact dose.
  5. Wipe injection site with a fresh alcohol swab. Let dry.
  6. Pinch a fold of skin (about 1–2 inches). Insert the needle at a 45° angle for lean areas or 90° if there's adequate subcutaneous fat.
  7. Inject slowly — 5–10 seconds for small volumes, 10–20 seconds for GHK-Cu or the Pinealon 100-unit dose.
  8. Withdraw the needle and apply gentle pressure with a clean cotton ball or tissue. Do not massage the site.
  9. Dispose of the syringe immediately in a sharps container. Never recap.

Common Questions

Q: Can I combine more peptides in the same syringe?

A: Semax + KPV + GHK-Cu (AM triple combo): Yes — all three are compatible. Draw KPV (6u) first, then Semax (12u), then GHK-Cu (20u) for a total of 38 units. Tested and confirmed. GHK-Cu's copper ion does not precipitate Semax or KPV.
Pinealon + DSIP (PM combo): Yes — draw DSIP (6u) first, then Pinealon (50u). Total: 56 units. Both target sleep/circadian pathways. Tesamorelin: Must remain separate — different pH and receptor pathway. Tesamorelin + anything: Keep separate. Tesamorelin requires fasted state and has a different pH profile. Never combine Tesamorelin with other peptides in the same syringe. Pinealon: Keep separate due to large volume.

Q: What if I miss a dose?

A: For morning peptides — if it's past 11 AM, skip and resume the next day. Never double-dose. For evening peptides — if you forget and it's past midnight, skip. For Pinealon during a 20-day course — take the missed dose as soon as you remember unless it's within 8 hours of the next dose.

Q: How long until I see results?

A: Sleep: DSIP works on night one. Pinealon melatonin effects build over 1–2 weeks. Gut: KPV — noticeable anti-inflammatory effect in 7–14 days. Hair: GHK-Cu — visible changes at 4–8 weeks (hair growth cycles are slow). Tesamorelin GH-driven hair benefits at 8–12 weeks.

Q: Can I use these peptides orally instead of injecting?

A: KPV can be orally active. Everything else — Semax, Tesamorelin, GHK-Cu, DSIP, Pinealon — must be injected subcutaneously. Oral bioavailability for these is near zero. (Semax has an intranasal formulation, but subQ is the standard for this protocol.)

Q: Do I need lab work?

A: Ideally, yes — especially for the GH axis peptide (Tesamorelin). Recommended labs: IGF-1, fasting glucose, HbA1c, liver panel (ALT/AST), and a CBC. Check at baseline and every 3 months. Tesamorelin can raise IGF-1 and slightly affect glucose tolerance — worth monitoring. Semax is non-hormonal and does not require specific lab monitoring.
