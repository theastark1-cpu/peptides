Step 1: Reconstitution

Each vial gets bacteriostatic water based on the desired concentration (see Quick Reference above).
Supplies needed: 3 mL mixing syringe with 18–23G needle, bacteriostatic water, alcohol wipes, Sharpie for labeling.

  1. Sanitize vial top with alcohol wipe. Let dry.
  2. Draw BAC water into mixing syringe to the correct volume (use the Quick Reference table above).
  3. Inject BAC water slowly into the peptide vial — aim the stream at the glass wall, not directly at the powder.
  4. Swirl gently — do NOT shake. Peptides are fragile proteins.
  5. Let sit 5–10 minutes until fully dissolved. Can also leave in the fridge overnight.
  6. Label immediately with peptide name, reconstitution date, and concentration (mg/mL).
  7. Store in refrigerator at 2–8°C (35–46°F). Protect from light.

💡 Pro tip: Reconstitute the night before your first dose so everything is settled and ready by morning. GHK-Cu will have a distinctive blue tint (normal — it's the copper ion). DSIP may appear slightly cloudy (also normal).

Dosing: 333 mcg (10 units) every other morning. Can be taken with or without food.

Supplies Checklist

  • U-100 insulin syringes — 31G, 5/16" or 8mm needle, 1 mL capacity. Include 3 additional separate syringes weekly for glutathione (2) and Reta (1). Buy in boxes of 100.
  • Bacteriostatic water — 30 mL multi-dose vials. Estimate 2–3 bottles for initial reconstitution of the peptide stack plus Reta; glutathione uses 5 mL BAC water. Keep a spare.
  • Alcohol swabs — 2 per injection (vial stopper wipe + skin prep). ~12–14 per day. Buy bulk boxes of 200+.
  • Sharps container — FDA-approved, puncture-resistant. Available at any pharmacy.
  • Vial storage case — Insulin vial organizer or small cosmetic fridge organizer to keep reconstituted vials upright and organized in the refrigerator.
  • Extra mixing syringes — 3 mL syringes with 21G–23G needles for BAC water transfer. Do not use insulin syringes for reconstitution — the fine needle damages the peptide.
  • Labels / Sharpie — Label every vial with reconstitution date and concentration immediately.
  • Refrigerator thermometer — Verify fridge stays at 2–8°C (35–46°F). Peptides degrade rapidly above 8°C.
  • Injection log / notebook — Track daily doses, injection sites, and any reactions or effects.

How to Inject

  1. Wash hands thoroughly with soap and warm water.
  2. Wipe vial stopper with an alcohol swab. Let dry completely (10–15 seconds).
  3. Draw air into the syringe equal to your dose volume. Inject air into the vial to equalize pressure.
  4. Draw the dose slowly. Flick the syringe to dislodge bubbles, then push plunger gently to expel air. Confirm exact dose.
  5. Wipe injection site with a fresh alcohol swab. Let dry.
  6. Pinch a fold of skin (about 1–2 inches). Insert the needle at a 45° angle for lean areas or 90° if there's adequate subcutaneous fat.
  7. Inject slowly — 5–10 seconds for small volumes, 10–20 seconds for GHK-Cu or the Pinealon 50-unit dose.
  8. Withdraw the needle and apply gentle pressure with a clean cotton ball or tissue. Do not massage the site.
  9. Dispose of the syringe immediately in a sharps container. Never recap.

Common Questions

Q: How do Reta and glutathione fit the EOD schedule?

A: They sit outside the alternating core schedule. Glutathione is Tuesday and Saturday at 300 mg. Reta is Sunday once weekly using the 10/20/30/40u titration. Both always use separate syringes.

Q: Should Reta automatically reach 2 mg?

A: No. Each step lasts four weeks, and escalation only occurs if the current dose is tolerated and no longer providing adequate appetite control. Persistent GI effects, dehydration, or excessive weight loss mean hold or reduce.

Q: Can I combine more peptides in the same syringe?

A: Semax + KPV + GHK-Cu (AM triple combo): Yes — all three are compatible. Draw KPV (5u) first, then Semax (10u), then GHK-Cu (20u) for a total of 35 units. Tested and confirmed. GHK-Cu's copper ion does not precipitate Semax or KPV.
Pinealon + DSIP (PM combo): Yes — draw DSIP (4u) first, then Pinealon (50u). Total: 54 units. Both target sleep/circadian pathways. Tesamorelin: Must remain separate — different pH and receptor pathway. Never combine Tesamorelin with other peptides in the same syringe.

Q: What if I miss a dose?

A: For morning peptides — if it's past 11 AM, skip and resume the next day. Never double-dose. For evening peptides — if you forget and it's past midnight, skip. For Pinealon during a 30-day course — take the missed dose as soon as you remember unless it's within 8 hours of the next dose.

Q: How long until I see results?

A: Sleep: DSIP works on night one. Pinealon melatonin effects build over 1–2 weeks. Gut: KPV — noticeable anti-inflammatory effect in 7–14 days. Hair: GHK-Cu — visible changes at 4–8 weeks (hair growth cycles are slow). Tesamorelin GH-driven hair benefits at 8–12 weeks.

Q: Can I use these peptides orally instead of injecting?

A: KPV can be orally active. Everything else — Semax, Tesamorelin, GHK-Cu, DSIP, Pinealon — must be injected subcutaneously. Oral bioavailability for these is near zero. (Semax has an intranasal formulation, but subQ is the standard for this protocol.)

Q: Do I need lab work?

A: Ideally, yes — especially for the GH axis peptide (Tesamorelin). Recommended labs: IGF-1, fasting glucose, HbA1c, liver panel (ALT/AST), and a CBC. Check at baseline and every 3 months. Tesamorelin can raise IGF-1 and slightly affect glucose tolerance — worth monitoring. Semax is non-hormonal and does not require specific lab monitoring.
